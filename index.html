MRI Pelvic Phantom for Adaptive Radiotherapy

A senior design project integrating medical imaging hardware, MRI distortion physics, and correction software to support adaptive radiotherapy workflows.

Texas A&M University at Qatar Electrical & Computer Engineering Capstone · ECEN 403
Project summary. We are developing an MRI-compatible pelvic phantom that mimics realistic anatomy and motion. By pairing this phantom with a dedicated image-processing pipeline, we aim to quantify geometric distortion in MR images and evaluate correction strategies for adaptive radiotherapy planning and verification.

Project Overview

Objective

Our objective is to design, build, and validate an MRI-compatible pelvic phantom that can be used to characterise geometric distortion and motion effects in MRI-based adaptive radiotherapy.

The phantom will include clinically relevant structures and landmarks with known ground-truth geometry so that distortion can be measured quantitatively and used to evaluate correction algorithms.

Motivation

  • Non-uniform magnetic fields, gradient nonlinearities, and susceptibility effects can distort MR images.
  • Adaptive radiotherapy workflows depend on high-fidelity MRI for target localization and dose calculation.
  • A standardized pelvic phantom enables repeatable experiments, quality assurance, and benchmarking of software tools.

Key Design Themes

  • Realistic pelvic anatomy with MRI-compatible materials.
  • Modular design allowing different organ configurations and motion scenarios.
  • Integrated workflow from data acquisition to distortion metrics and correction.
  • Close collaboration with medical physicists at HMC and NCCCR.

Phantom Design & Hardware

Geometry & Anatomy

  • Pelvic bone shell derived from anatomical references (CT/MR datasets or literature models).
  • Internal compartments representing bladder, rectum, target volumes, and surrounding soft tissue.
  • Embedded fiducial markers and grid patterns for precise distortion tracking.
  • Provision for inserting different inserts (e.g., different tissue-mimicking materials).

Materials & Manufacturing

  • MRI-compatible materials with controlled relaxation properties (T1/T2) where possible.
  • 3D-printed structures with sealed volumes for liquid or gel filling.
  • Modular assembly to simplify cleaning, refilling, and future upgrades.
  • Design constraints informed by scanner bore size and clinical workflow at NCCCR.

Motion & Scenario Modules

To support adaptive workflows, the phantom is planned with optional motion modules:

  • Simple translation and rotation stages to simulate patient setup shifts.
  • Breathing-like or organ-motion inserts (where feasible) for dynamic studies.
  • Repeatable reference positions to enable before/after comparison across scans.

Distortion Analysis & Correction Software

Image Processing Pipeline

  • Acquisition of phantom MR images under different sequences and scanner settings.
  • Segmentation / registration of known structures and fiducials.
  • Computation of displacement fields by comparing measured positions to ground-truth geometry.
  • Generation of distortion heatmaps and quantitative metrics (e.g., maximum displacement, RMS error).

Correction Strategies

  • Exploring scanner-provided corrections vs. custom post-processing.
  • Testing geometry-based warping methods to “undo” measured distortions.
  • Evaluating impact of correction on target localization and dose mapping.
  • Preparing a report framework that can be reused by clinical physicists for QA.

Implementation tools may include Python (NumPy, SciPy, SimpleITK), MATLAB, or other image-processing environments allowed by the course and clinical partners.

Validation & Evaluation Plan

Planned Experiments

  • Baseline scans on the target MRI system(s) at NCCCR with multiple sequences (T1, T2, distortion-sensitive protocols).
  • Comparison between vendor distortion correction options and our custom analysis pipeline.
  • Reproducibility studies: repeated scans with controlled setup changes.
  • Sensitivity analysis: how distortion varies with sequence parameters and phantom position.

Evaluation Metrics

  • Maximum geometric error across the imaging volume.
  • Mean and RMS distortion within regions of interest.
  • Effect of distortion on target contour alignment and dose grid mapping (where available).
  • Qualitative feedback from medical physicists at HMC/NCCCR.
